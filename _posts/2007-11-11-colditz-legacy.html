--- layout: post title: The Colditz Legacy date: '2007-11-11T13:32:00.000+08:00' author: Paul Gallagher tags: - Read - WWII modified_time: '2007-11-11T14:01:52.324+08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-18987903.post-1376616421292201556 blogger_orig_url: http://pratalife.blogspot.com/2007/11/colditz-legacy.html --- Guy Walters' The Colditz Legacy is an engaging read. For those of a certain age and cultural background, Colditz (and especially escape thereof) epitomises a grand Boy's Own tradition of daring and adventure. Walters picked a great backdrop for his story which features Colditz during WWII and 30 years on during the cold war. But this story is not about Colditz itself, but the two main characters; men thrown together during the war which each survived in their own way.

When I started the book, I assumed the "legacy" must mean something like nazi loot or some deep secret, but it is much more subtle. There is the idea that in our past we may have performed in way that meets all external, objective measures of approval but in our hearts is in some way unsatisfactory. This is the legacy we carry around, even subconsciously. Few may want or get the chance to revisit and rectify this conflict in their lifetime. This is the story of one man who does.

I like Guy Walters' writing. I'm sure this won't be the last book of his I read, in fact I have just picked up The Leader.
