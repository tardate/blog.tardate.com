--- layout: post title: The Best Software Writing I date: '2007-10-18T00:01:00.000+08:00' author: Paul Gallagher tags: - Laugh - TechRead - Read modified_time: '2007-10-18T00:19:15.197+08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-18987903.post-201417196556636027 blogger_orig_url: http://pratalife.blogspot.com/2007/10/best-software-writing-i.html --- The Best Software Writing I .. it's a pity that there was never a volume II for this classic collection of the best software writing, selected and introduced by Joel Spolsky.

I just picked it up to re-read some of my favourites, like
  • Bruce Eckel's Strong Typing vs. Strong Testing, which is a bit of a milestone article in the rise of the "scripting" languages like ruby and Python.
  • Adam Bosworth's ICSOC04 Talk which reminds us it is more important for computer science to be understandable by people than by computers.

There's also some great pointed humour, with
  • Rory Blyth's Excel as a Database
  • Powerpoint Remix - Aaron Swartz
  • And the legendary A Quick (and Hopefully Painless) Ride Through Ruby (with Cartoon Foxes) by why the lucky stiff

Definitely one of the all-time-must-have tech reads (and re-reads).
