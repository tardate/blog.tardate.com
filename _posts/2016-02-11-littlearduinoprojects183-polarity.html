--- layout: post title: LEAP#183 Polarity Testing date: '2016-02-11T22:40:00.001+08:00' author: Paul Gallagher tags: - Electronics modified_time: '2016-02-14T22:23:15.082+08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6220586.post-4286635894681251053 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.tardate.com/2016/02/littlearduinoprojects183-polarity.html --- A simple polarity test for uses a series of inverters. The input signal is pumped into two parallel inverter chains:
  • a single inverter
  • a series of two inverters
So, regardless of input polarity, one chain output will be high and the other low.

I used a venerable CD4069 for a quick test, although any inverter (matched to the voltage of the signal) will do. The result of the polarity test is displayed with a pair of LEDs reversed in parallel.
