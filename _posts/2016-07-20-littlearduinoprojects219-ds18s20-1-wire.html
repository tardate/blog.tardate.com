--- layout: post title: LEAP#219 DS18S20 1-Wire Reading date: '2016-07-20T23:50:00.000+08:00' author: Paul Gallagher tags: - LittleArduinoProjects - Sensors - Arduino - Electronics modified_time: '2016-07-20T23:50:33.440+08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6220586.post-2898443714676284711 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.tardate.com/2016/07/littlearduinoprojects219-ds18s20-1-wire.html --- Another way to measure temperatures, this time the DS18S20 - an inexpensive 1-Wire digital thermometer with a range of -55°C to +125°C, at ±0.5°C accuracy from -10°C to +85°C.

It comes in TO-92 packaging and may be powered directly (using three pins), or take parasitic power from the data line (using two pins). This makes it ideal for pin-starved microcontrollers that need a decent ambient temperature reading. In this project, I'm just exercising the sensor with a simple Arduino sketch.

As always, all notes, schematics and code are in the Little Electronics & Arduino Projects repo on GitHub.
